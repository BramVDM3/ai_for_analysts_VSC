# Interview Transcript: Senior Member

## Context

- Interview type: Semi-structured stakeholder interview
- Interview date: 2026-05-08
- Interviewer: Business analyst
- Participant: Senior library member
- Role: Retired member, regular borrower, attends occasional reading activities
- Current system discussed: Library lending process and current web application used by staff

## Transcript

**Interviewer:** Thank you for speaking with me. Could you tell me how you normally use the library?

**Senior Member:** I come every two or three weeks. I borrow novels mostly, sometimes history books. I also come for the reading group when it is something I like. For me the library is not only about books. It is part of my routine.

**Interviewer:** Do you use the library application yourself?

**Senior Member:** No, not really. I know there is a website, but I usually ask at the desk. The librarians know me. They can find things faster than I can. If I try to search myself, I worry I am pressing the wrong thing.

**Interviewer:** What do you think of the current lending process?

**Senior Member:** At the desk it is usually easy. I give them my card and the books, and they do the rest. That is how I like it. I do not mind waiting a little if I know a person will help me. The process feels safe because there is someone to ask.

**Interviewer:** Do you usually need the full senior lending period?

**Senior Member:** Not really. I know seniors can keep books longer, and I appreciate that it exists, but I usually bring them back after about a week. I come often anyway. The longer period is nice for holidays or illness, but for normal reading it is not the main reason I borrow. I would not want the library to make everything more complicated just because seniors technically have more days.

**Interviewer:** Have you had any problems with loans or returns?

**Senior Member:** Sometimes I am told a book cannot be borrowed, even though it is on the shelf or in the catalogue. The librarian explains if it is only for reference, but I do not always understand why it appears available then. Available should mean available to take home, at least to me.

**Interviewer:** How do you keep track of due dates?

**Senior Member:** I write them down. Sometimes the librarian tells me, and sometimes I keep the receipt. I do not always check email. If I forget, I only find out when I come back. I do not like surprises with money.

**Interviewer:** Would reminders help?

**Senior Member:** Yes, but not only on a phone. I do not use notifications the way young people do. An email is fine if it is clear. A printed reminder would be even better, but I know that may be old-fashioned. Maybe a phone call for people who choose it? I know that is more work.

**Interviewer:** What do you think about overdue charges?

**Senior Member:** I understand there must be rules. Otherwise people keep books forever. But I want to know the amount before it becomes a problem. If there is a charge, the librarian should be able to explain it without doing arithmetic in front of me.

**Interviewer:** Has that happened?

**Senior Member:** Once. It was not a big amount, but the librarian had to look at the loan, then another screen, then count the days. I felt bad because there was a queue. I also felt a bit foolish, as if I should have known. A clear total would be helpful, but I also want to know what it is made of.

**Interviewer:** Would you want to see that calculation yourself in an app?

**Senior Member:** Maybe on a large screen, but not only there. If the librarian can show me or print it, that is enough. I do not want to be told, "You can see it in the app," because then I have to solve two problems instead of one.

**Interviewer:** There has been discussion about self-checkout. How would you feel about that?

**Senior Member:** I would be worried. I know some people like doing everything themselves, but I do not want the desk to disappear. Machines are fine until something goes wrong. When something goes wrong, I need a person.

**Interviewer:** What worries you most?

**Senior Member:** Making a mistake. Borrowing the wrong book, not finishing the loan, being charged incorrectly, or thinking I returned something when the system says I did not. If a librarian handles it, I trust it more. If I have to scan things myself, I will wonder whether I did it correctly.

**Interviewer:** Would you ever use self-checkout if it were optional?

**Senior Member:** Perhaps if someone showed me slowly and it was very clear. But optional must mean optional. Sometimes new systems are "optional" for a year and then suddenly everyone is expected to use them. That would make me come less often.

**Interviewer:** What do you like about the current application, from what you see at the desk?

**Senior Member:** The librarians can find loans quickly most of the time. They do not have to search through paper. When it works, it is calm. I like that they can see whether a loan is active or returned. I just do not want the screen to become something that takes their attention away from the person in front of them.

**Interviewer:** You mentioned reading groups. Would you like more community features in the application?

**Senior Member:** I like knowing what activities are happening. A calendar would be nice. But again, not only in an app. Put it on the website, print it, have the librarian mention it. I do not want community to mean digital only. The community is here, in the building.

**Interviewer:** The director has talked about different membership levels and different prices for different services. How would that feel to you?

**Senior Member:** Complicated. I already have a senior membership. If there are more levels, I would ask what I am losing. Libraries should be simple. I understand events may cost something, but borrowing books should not feel like choosing a telephone subscription.

**Interviewer:** Would you pay more for extra services?

**Senior Member:** Maybe for a special event, not for basic borrowing. I would not like discovering that a certain type of book has a different price after I brought it to the desk. Tell me first, clearly, and keep it reasonable.

**Interviewer:** What information should the librarian have when helping you?

**Senior Member:** They should see my current loans, due dates, and whether I owe anything. If I am blocked, they should know why, but I do not want private information displayed loudly. Sometimes people stand close in line. A little discretion matters.

**Interviewer:** Do you feel comfortable discussing blocks or charges at the desk?

**Senior Member:** Not always. Money is private. Being told you cannot borrow is embarrassing, even if the reason is simple. I prefer when the librarian can say it quietly and confidently. If they have to search and look confused, I become anxious.

**Interviewer:** What would make the app better for you?

**Senior Member:** Bigger text would help. Clear words. Not too many colors. If something needs attention, say what it is. If there is a date, make it obvious. If there is a charge, show the amount and why. And please keep the desk.

**Interviewer:** What would be your biggest concern about future changes?

**Senior Member:** That the library becomes less human. I know technology is useful. I am not against it. But I do not want to feel that I am too slow for the library. The app should help the librarian help me, not replace the librarian.

**Interviewer:** Anything you particularly appreciate today?

**Senior Member:** The staff. And the fact that the system is not too complicated at the desk. I can still hand over my books and have a normal conversation. If improvements keep that feeling, I am happy.
